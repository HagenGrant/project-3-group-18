Tornadoes by Year

Tornadoes are one of nature's most violent and unpredictable phenomena, characterized by their powerful rotating columns of air that extend from thunderstorms to the ground. These intense spirals of wind can reach speeds of up to 300 miles per hour, causing devastating destruction along their paths. Typically forming in regions where warm, moist air meets cold, dry air, tornadoes are most common in the United States, particularly in an area known as "Tornado Alley." This region includes parts of Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas, and Nebraska. Tornadoes can vary greatly in size and duration, with some lasting mere minutes and others persisting for over an hour.
